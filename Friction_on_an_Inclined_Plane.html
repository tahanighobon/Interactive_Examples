Key equations

Weight = mg

Normal force = mg cosθ

Downslope force = mg sinθ

Maximum friction = μN

a = (mg sinθ - μmg cosθ) / m
The block stays at rest when friction can fully balance the downslope component of gravity.

What students should notice

  • As the incline angle increases, the downslope component of gravity increases.
  • As friction increases, the block is more likely to stay at rest.
  • Mass changes both gravity and normal force, so the condition for sliding depends mainly on angle and friction.
  • The block begins to slide when the downslope force becomes greater than the maximum friction force.

A very good classroom question is: why does changing the mass not change whether the block starts sliding, when friction and gravity both depend on mass?
