What students should notice

  • When wind speed doubles, power does not just double. It increases much more because of the cubic relationship.
  • Larger blade radius means a larger swept area, so the turbine can capture more wind energy.
  • The power coefficient shows that only part of the wind power can be captured by the rotor.
  • If wind speed is below cut-in or above cut-out, the turbine does not generate power.

A strong classroom activity is to keep all variables fixed and change only wind speed, then ask students why small speed changes produce such large power changes.
