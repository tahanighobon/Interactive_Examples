What students should notice

  • A smaller k makes the model more sensitive to nearby individual points.
  • A larger k makes the model smoother and less sensitive to local noise.
  • The decision regions change shape depending on the training data and k value.
  • The predicted class is based on the majority class among the nearest neighbors.
