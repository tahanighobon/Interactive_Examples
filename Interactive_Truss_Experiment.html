Quick Question

If the load increases, which member force increases the most?

In this symmetric triangular truss, all member forces increase when the load increases. The inclined members AB and BC usually carry larger force magnitudes than the bottom member AC.

Live Truss Model

What students should notice

  • The top load is shared by the two supports.
  • The two inclined members carry compression.
  • The bottom member carries tension.
  • Changing the truss height changes the internal forces.

Key relationships

Ay = Cy = P / 2

F_AB = F_BC = P / (2 sinθ)

F_AC = P / (2 tanθ)

Where θ is the angle of the inclined member with the horizontal.
